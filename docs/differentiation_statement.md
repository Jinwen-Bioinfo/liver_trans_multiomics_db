# Differentiation Statement

## Working Claim

LiverTx-OmicsDB is a transplant-specific, value-added multi-omics evidence resource for liver transplantation. It integrates public data from broad repositories and normalizes them around clinical transplant concepts that generic omics repositories do not curate consistently.

## Why Existing Repositories Are Not Enough

| Resource | Strength | Gap for liver transplantation users |
| --- | --- | --- |
| GEO/SRA | Primary transcriptomics and sequencing archive | No transplant-specific clinical state harmonization, cross-study rejection signatures, or gene/cell-type evidence pages |
| NGDC GSA-Human | Important human omics accessions from China and global studies | Metadata is accession-centered, not transplant biology-centered |
| PRIDE | Proteomics archive | Not integrated with liver transplant transcriptomics, cell types, or clinical rejection labels |
| MetaboLights | Metabolomics archive | No graft-state-centered linkage to genes, pathways, or transplant phases |
| MGnify | Microbiome archive and analysis | No liver transplant immune or graft outcome evidence model |
| Human Protein Atlas | Tissue and protein expression reference | Not transplant-specific and not linked to rejection or donor liver quality contrasts |

## What LiverTx-OmicsDB Adds

1. **Transplant-specific curation**
   Public studies are normalized by graft state, transplant phase, sample origin, and rejection/injury phenotype.

2. **Cross-study evidence**
   Users can query a gene, pathway, cell type, or clinical state across multiple accessions.

3. **Multi-omics evidence graph**
   Bulk RNA, single-cell RNA, pathology metadata, proteomics, metabolomics, and microbiome datasets are linked through shared biological entities.

4. **Transparent provenance**
   Every result points back to source accession, sample labels, processing version, and QC status.

5. **Clinically meaningful entry points**
   The portal starts from liver transplant questions: rejection, donor liver quality, graft injury, fibrosis, tolerance, and blood monitoring.

## NAR Submission Angle

The broad value is not limited to surgeons or hepatologists. The resource also serves immunologists, cell biologists, systems biologists, and computational researchers studying human graft rejection, tissue-resident immunity, inflammatory injury, and organ-quality molecular phenotypes.
