# Glossary

## Evidence Grade A

Reusable sample-level matrix or directly queryable cohort table with defensible contrast reconstruction.

## Evidence Grade B

Reusable feature-level or group-level processed evidence with structured contrasts but not a full reusable sample matrix.

## Evidence Grade C

Marker-level or figure-recovered evidence that is still useful, but narrower and less reusable than sample-level or feature-level layers.

## Evidence Grade R

Reference context used for interpretation rather than direct transplant outcome inference.

## Evidence Grade M

Metadata-only or unresolved candidate layer that informs prioritization but does not yet provide downloadable evidence.

## Claim boundary

The explicit statement of what the current evidence can support and what remains out of scope.

## Demonstrator use case

A question-first workflow chosen to prove that the resource can deliver an end-to-end scientific narrative, not just dataset lookup.

## Sample origin

The biological material source for a dataset or evidence layer, such as donor liver biopsy, graft liver biopsy, blood, serum, plasma, PBMC, stool, or bile.

## Direct transplant evidence

A dataset or layer derived from liver-transplant-relevant samples and labels, rather than a generic liver reference context.

## Reference context

A contextual layer that helps interpret a signal, such as cell-type or liver reference proteomics, without directly supporting transplant outcome claims.

## Provenance

The record of where a source came from, how it was downloaded, processed, normalized, and exposed in the resource.

## Quickstart journey

A curated path through use-case, study, and feature views that helps a user understand a scientific question with minimal wandering.

## Reviewer walkthrough

A structured set of checkpoints intended for internal reviewers, collaborators, or manuscript reviewers to verify the resource's main differentiators quickly.
